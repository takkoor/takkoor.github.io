Designing a Policy Evaluation Framework

A visual decision-support tool for evaluating education policies through equity, structure, and long-term impact

Context

Education policies are often evaluated using fragmented or inconsistent criteria, making it difficult to assess whether they meaningfully improve outcomes across diverse communities.

Students and policymakers alike struggle to move beyond opinion-based arguments toward structured, equity-aware evaluation.

Problem

How might we create a clear, repeatable framework that helps people evaluate education policies holistically—without oversimplifying complex social realities?

Audience

  • Policy students
  • Educators
  • Advocates and researchers
  • Decision-makers evaluating education initiatives

Constraints

  • Had to function as a single-page reference
  • Needed to be usable without facilitation
  • Required balancing historical context with modern policy needs
  • Had to center equity without isolating it as a standalone concern

Design Goals

  • Translate abstract policy principles into actionable criteria
  • Encourage structured, comparative evaluation
  • Make equity and inclusion foundational rather than optional
  • Support long-term thinking instead of short-term outcomes

Key Design Decisions

Why seven standards?

The framework balances cognitive load with completeness—enough criteria to capture complexity without overwhelming users.

Why a vertical, sequential layout?

The structure encourages users to move through evaluation intentionally, reinforcing that effective policy must meet multiple conditions simultaneously.

Why embedded historical context?

Historical examples ground abstract criteria in real outcomes, reinforcing that policy decisions have long-term, systemic consequences.

Why repeated “WHY?” prompts?

These callouts nudge users to interrogate assumptions rather than accept surface-level success metrics.

Outcome & Impact

  • Used as a teaching and discussion tool in education policy contexts
  • Helped users shift from opinion-driven to criteria-driven analysis
  • Encouraged equity-first thinking in policy evaluation

Reflection

This framework could evolve into an interactive checklist or decision-support tool, allowing users to compare policies dynamically or apply the standards in real time.
